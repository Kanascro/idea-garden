## Core idea

Fulfilling relational agreements emerge through ongoing negotiation rather than automatic acceptance of either partner’s initial expectations.

## Structure

Expectations around time, attention, intimacy, and commitment can evolve through discussion rather than fixed rules. Negotiation allows both partners to express needs and concerns while exploring creative compromises that support mutual well-being. Critical reflection on expectations helps identify which desires reflect essential needs and which arise from habit, fear, or cultural assumptions. The goal is not to satisfy every preference but to design relational structures that remain sustainable and fair for both individuals.

## Human signal

Partnership begins to feel collaborative when expectations are shaped together.

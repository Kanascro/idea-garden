## Core idea

Framing perceptions as universal truths creates separation, while expressing them as personal experiences preserves connection.

## Structure

Statements that present preferences as objective (“this is best”) impose implicit agreement and generate friction. In contrast, expressing experience as subjective (“I like this”) allows difference without conflict. The shift removes pressure to align, making interaction more fluid and less adversarial.

## Human signal

Connection feels easier when nothing needs to be proven.

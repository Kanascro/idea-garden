## Core idea

The capacity to feel joy for a partner’s experiences grows when one’s own life feels full, autonomous, and emotionally supported.

## Structure

Compersion becomes difficult when personal needs for adventure, connection, intimacy, or creative expression remain unmet. When life feels constrained, a partner’s external relationships can appear to highlight that lack. In contrast, when individuals maintain their own sources of vitality—friendships, exploration, sensuality, creativity, independence—the partner’s experiences no longer threaten personal fulfillment. Emotional abundance reduces comparison and makes space for genuine appreciation of the partner’s happiness.

## Human signal

Joy for another person becomes easier when life already feels rich and expansive.

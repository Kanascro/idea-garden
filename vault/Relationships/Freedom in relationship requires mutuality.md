## Core idea

Relational freedom is not unilateral autonomy but the coexistence of multiple desires that must be negotiated.

## Structure

Each person retains the right to pursue what they want, yet relationships must determine whether those desires can coexist. When preferences or pacing differ, neither automatically overrides the other. The relationship continues through negotiation, adaptation, or honest recognition of incompatibility.

## Human signal

Freedom becomes collaborative rather than solitary.

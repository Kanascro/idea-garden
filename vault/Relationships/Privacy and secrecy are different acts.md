## Core idea

Privacy protects information that is not solely one’s to share, while secrecy hides one’s own truth out of fear.

## Structure

Privacy involves withholding details that belong partly to others or that are not necessary to disclose. Secrecy occurs when someone suppresses their own experiences or feelings to avoid conflict or judgment. It is possible to share one’s emotional reality without exposing information that belongs to someone else.

## Human signal

Honesty about one’s inner world can coexist with respect for others’ boundaries.

## Core idea

A boundary does not dictate another person’s behavior; it defines what situations one is willing to remain in.

## Structure

Control attempts to regulate what others are allowed to do. A boundary instead communicates personal limits and the conditions for continued participation. It clarifies what environments, dynamics, or behaviors someone can stay present for and what they cannot. Others remain free to act as they choose; the boundary simply determines whether one continues participating.

## Human signal

The power of a boundary lies in self-respect: it names where one’s capacity ends.

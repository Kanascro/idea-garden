## Core idea

Fear emerges when partners interpret the same behavior through incompatible meanings and motivations.

## Structure

One partner may pursue openness as exploration, autonomy, or personal authenticity. The other may interpret the same behavior as urgency, dissatisfaction, or emotional distance. When motivations remain unclear or difficult to understand, trust erodes because the relational signals become ambiguous. Clarity about why certain desires exist—and how they relate to the relationship itself—helps reduce this uncertainty. Without shared understanding, partners may experience the same situation as entirely different emotional realities.

## Human signal

Fear often reflects confusion rather than rejection: the mind trying to understand what the other person’s behavior truly means.

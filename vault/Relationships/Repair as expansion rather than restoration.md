## Core idea

Repairing a relationship can involve strengthening and redesigning its structure rather than simply returning to a previous state.

## Structure

When relational tension reveals hidden dynamics—such as unmet needs, unclear boundaries, or imbalanced emotional labor—the repair process offers an opportunity to reconfigure the partnership. Instead of restoring the relationship to its earlier form, partners can integrate new insights into more resilient patterns of communication, autonomy, and connection. This process transforms conflict into a catalyst for relational evolution.

## Human signal

A repaired relationship often feels more spacious and intentional than the version that existed before the rupture.

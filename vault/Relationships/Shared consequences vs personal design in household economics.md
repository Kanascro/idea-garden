## Core idea

When institutions treat two individuals as a single household, financial consequences can become structurally shared even if the individuals remain fiscally independent.

## Structure

Two valid interpretations can exist. In a **shared structural cost** approach, financial effects created by the system’s classification are treated as collective outcomes that partners rebalance together. In a **personal design ownership** approach, financial impacts are carried individually when they arise from personal choices, such as intentionally pursuing a lower-income path.

## Human signal

The real question is not only about money but about how responsibility is defined when private lives intersect with institutional systems.

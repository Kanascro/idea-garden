## Core idea

Framing actions in terms of “permission” introduces hierarchy, while voluntary agreements create relational balance.

## Structure

Permission implies that one partner holds authority to allow or forbid the other’s actions, establishing an implicit power structure. Agreements, by contrast, are mutually chosen understandings that both partners commit to voluntarily. Either person can revisit or end the agreement through open dialogue rather than through control.

## Human signal

Relationships feel more empowering when choices arise from mutual willingness rather than from granted authority.

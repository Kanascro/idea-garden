## Core idea

Relationships often become strained when individuals anticipate a partner’s needs or fears without explicitly confirming them.

## Structure

Protective assumptions arise from care and concern but can gradually replace direct communication. One partner may limit behavior, suppress desires, or structure daily life according to imagined expectations. This internal management attempts to avoid conflict or insecurity but often creates invisible constraints. When expectations remain unspoken, both partners operate on interpretations rather than shared agreements. Replacing assumption with explicit dialogue allows boundaries, desires, and reassurances to be negotiated consciously rather than silently enforced.

## Human signal

Relational clarity emerges when imagined expectations are replaced with spoken agreements.

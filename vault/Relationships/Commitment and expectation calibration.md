## Core idea

Relationships benefit when personal expectations about commitment are consciously examined rather than assumed.

## Structure

Individuals often carry implicit expectations about the level of emotional investment, stability, and long-term planning within a relationship. These expectations may not match those of the partner, especially in relationships exploring non-traditional structures such as openness. Clarifying what commitment means to each person—emotionally, practically, and temporally—helps align expectations with reality. When expectations remain unconscious, disappointment or confusion can arise even when neither partner has violated explicit agreements.

## Human signal

Security grows when commitment is defined through shared understanding rather than silent assumptions.

## Core idea

Emotional safety in a relationship often depends on whether one’s feelings are acknowledged and treated as legitimate.

## Structure

When a partner dismisses, minimizes, or repeatedly questions someone’s emotional experience, the nervous system can register that environment as unsafe. Validation does not require agreement with every interpretation; it simply means recognizing that the other person’s feelings exist and deserve space. Without that recognition, trust and openness gradually erode.

## Human signal

People relax and open when their inner world is welcomed instead of debated.

## Core idea

Jealousy can emerge not from a partner’s freedom but from self-imposed restriction that creates an internal sense of deprivation.

## Structure

When one partner unconsciously limits their own exploration, pleasure, or autonomy in order to protect the other’s feelings, a scarcity dynamic can develop. The individual sacrifices parts of their life to maintain the partner’s sense of security. Over time this creates an imbalance: personal needs go unmet while emotional energy becomes increasingly invested in the partner’s behavior. This deprivation can lead to subtle resentment or jealousy because the partner appears freer than the self. The emotional tension is therefore generated less by the partner’s actions and more by unacknowledged self-limitation.

## Human signal

The feeling of jealousy often masks a quieter experience of being undernourished by one’s own life.

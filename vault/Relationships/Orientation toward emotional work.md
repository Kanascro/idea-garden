## Core idea

Compatibility depends on whether partners share—or can respect—similar orientations toward emotional processing.

## Structure

One partner approaches emotions as something to actively work with, regulate, and metabolize, while the other may tend to endure or move past emotional states without structured engagement. This difference becomes sustainable only if it does not require one person to shrink their needs. Emotional overwhelm must be something the relationship can address intentionally, through practices or support that feel authentic to both partners.

## Human signal

The hope remains to be proven wrong. The request is not for sameness, but for space where emotional depth can exist without self-contraction.

## Core idea

When individuals reclaim alignment in relationships, emotional change often follows a recognizable sequence of pause, grief, reorganization, and eventual forward movement.

## Structure

The first stage occurs when truth is spoken and previous adaptations are released. This often produces a temporary pause as the nervous system processes the implications. Grief follows as expectations and imagined futures dissolve. During this period the psyche reorganizes its sense of identity, agency, and relational boundaries. Only after this internal restructuring stabilizes does forward motion become possible, whether through renewed partnership under new conditions or through separation. Each phase serves a regulatory function that helps integrate clarity without overwhelming the individual.

## Human signal

Stillness and detachment are not signs of failure in the process. They are the system gathering strength before the next step emerges.

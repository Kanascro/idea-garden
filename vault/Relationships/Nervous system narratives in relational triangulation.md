## Core idea

When distress is followed by a partner turning toward someone else, the nervous system can encode a pattern: _my vulnerability leads to my displacement._

## Structure

A sequence formed in which emotional upset coincided with the partner spending time with a specific third person. Even unrelated moments—such as declining an activity due to being upset—became linked to the expectation that the partner would instead go to that person. This creates a reinforcing loop where distress becomes associated with relational abandonment or substitution.

## Human signal

The resulting feeling is not simple jealousy but a deeper fear: that showing difficulty or fragility makes one less chosen. The nervous system begins bracing for replacement whenever vulnerability appears.

## Core idea

Relational compatibility increases when partners value both stability and exploration rather than embodying only one extreme.

## Structure

Some individuals orient primarily toward constant novelty, ambition, and expansion, while others prioritize calm routines and emotional steadiness. Partnerships become strained when one orientation consistently dominates the relationship. Compatibility grows when both individuals appreciate a rhythm that includes quiet presence as well as intellectual or creative engagement. This balance allows relationships to function both as sanctuary and as support for personal growth.

## Human signal

The relationship becomes both a refuge and a place where new possibilities can emerge.

## Core idea

Letting go of certain emotions can feel frightening because it may transform the structure of a relationship.

## Structure

Emotions such as jealousy can function as psychological markers of exclusivity, priority, or attachment. When those emotions soften or disappear, it can feel as if the relationship itself is shifting into unfamiliar territory. The fear is not only about the emotion but about the new relational reality it might reveal.

## Human signal

Sometimes people cling to difficult feelings because they anchor a known version of love.

## Core idea

Physical practices such as movement, dance, sensory play, and voice can help restore vitality after emotional upheaval.

## Structure

Emotional transitions often disrupt the body’s sense of rhythm and safety. Activities like exercise, dancing, singing, and sensory interactions re-engage bodily systems associated with pleasure, regulation, and expression. These practices reconnect individuals with sensations of strength, aliveness, and autonomy. Rather than functioning as distraction, they serve as embodied reminders that the body remains a source of creativity and joy even when relational circumstances change.

## Human signal

The body becomes a partner in healing rather than a container for stress.

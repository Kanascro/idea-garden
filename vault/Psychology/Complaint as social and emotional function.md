## Core idea

Complaining serves multiple adaptive roles but does not inherently lead to change.

## Structure

It can release tension, seek validation, create social bonding, and signal desire for improvement. It can also become habitual or simulate control in situations that feel overwhelming. Without transition into action or reorientation, it stabilizes discomfort rather than resolving it.

## Human signal

There is empathy for why people complain, alongside a desire to move beyond it.

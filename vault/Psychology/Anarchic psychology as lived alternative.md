## Core idea

Psychological order can arise from presence and responsiveness rather than imposed containment.

## Structure

Conventional models assume the need for external frameworks to prevent fragmentation. This perspective challenges that assumption, proposing that imposed order may itself create disconnection. By removing rigid structures, a different form of organization appears—adaptive, emergent, and internally guided.

## Human signal

This is not abstract rebellion; it is a response to what has failed and a turn toward what feels alive.

## Core idea

Conditional worth systems operate as relational contracts: “I am worthy if…”. They are not inherently harmful; they function within specific social ecosystems.

## Structure

Different communities stabilize belonging and recognition through particular worth conditions. These contracts define what behaviors, identities, or roles maintain status inside the system. They can provide meaning, motivation, and cohesion, but they also create vulnerability when a person’s worth depends on maintaining a specific condition.

## Human signal

Conditional worth is not a failure of humanity. It is one of the ways social worlds organize recognition and belonging.

## Core idea

Complaining relieves pressure but also encodes unspoken desires.

## Structure

Expressions of frustration function as release, bonding, and coping in the face of overwhelm. They create connection and momentary relief without requiring resolution. Within each complaint lies implicit information about unmet needs or preferred alternatives, but this signal is often ignored in favor of repetition.

## Human signal

There is tenderness here: complaint is not failure, but an early, incomplete form of care.

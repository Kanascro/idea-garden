## Core idea

Recognizing another person’s confusion or sadness does not mean taking responsibility for resolving their internal process.

## Structure

When relational boundaries become clear, the other person may experience uncertainty, grief, or confusion as they face their own unresolved priorities and desires. These reactions are natural responses to confronting reality. However, clarity requires distinguishing between empathy and ownership. Each person must process their own emotions and make their own decisions about alignment, timing, and commitment. When responsibility remains correctly distributed, both individuals retain the freedom to examine their values and choose their path without coercion.

## Human signal

Care can remain present even while emotional labor is no longer carried for the other person.

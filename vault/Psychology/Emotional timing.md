## Core idea

Psychological integration follows its own pacing and cannot always be accelerated through deliberate behavioral strategies.

## Structure

Periods of emotional change—such as grief, identity transition, or relational reorganization—often prompt attempts to regain control through structured activities intended to hasten recovery. However, emotional systems reorganize through gradual processes that include reflection, rest, and spontaneous insight. Efforts to accelerate these processes can introduce additional strain by imposing expectations on experiences that unfold organically. Respecting emotional timing allows internal adjustments to stabilize without external pressure.

## Human signal

Relief often accompanies the removal of urgency around emotional resolution.

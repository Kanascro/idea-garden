## Core idea

Certain relational or environmental cues can trigger intense physiological reactions such as panic, anxiety, or intrusive thoughts.

## Structure

When the mind associates a cue with a perceived threat, the body can react immediately through stress responses before rational evaluation occurs. These reactions may include racing thoughts, urges to escape, catastrophic interpretations, and difficulty regulating emotions.

## Human signal

The body can experience danger even when the mind is still trying to understand what is happening.

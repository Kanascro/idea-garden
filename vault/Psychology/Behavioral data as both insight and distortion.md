## Core idea

Understanding people requires more than what they say, but observing behavior introduces its own biases and risks.

## Structure

Self-reported values are limited by self-deception, social desirability, and lack of awareness. Behavioral signals reveal patterns but can overreach, especially when treated as more “real” than lived experience. Any system must balance these sources without letting inference override self-definition.

## Human signal

The tension remains unresolved: people are not fully reliable narrators, but neither are the systems that interpret them.

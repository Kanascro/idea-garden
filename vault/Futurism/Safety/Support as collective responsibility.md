## Core idea

Healing from harm becomes a shared social task rather than a private burden carried by individuals alone.

## Structure

Traditional systems often isolate both the harmed and the person who caused harm. A capacity-based model instead mobilizes community support around both sides. Those affected receive resources and care until their adaptation load decreases. Those responsible receive guidance, education, and accountability structures that expand their behavioral options. This collective response recognizes that emotional recovery and behavioral change rarely occur in isolation. Communities function as stabilizing environments that absorb stress without allowing individuals to collapse under it.

## Human signal

No one is disposable, and no one is left to recover alone.

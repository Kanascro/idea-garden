## Core idea

Long-term safety emerges less from policing behavior and more from widespread relational skills that help people navigate needs, boundaries, and consent.

## Structure

Many harmful interactions occur because individuals lack the ability to recognize their own needs, communicate boundaries clearly, interpret others’ signals, or regulate emotional states during conflict. When these skills are widely taught and practiced, fewer collisions occur. Education in emotional literacy, consent communication, nervous-system awareness, and conflict repair becomes core social infrastructure. Communities become safer not because they eliminate risk entirely but because people can navigate relational complexity without overriding one another.

## Human signal

Safety begins to feel like competence rather than surveillance.

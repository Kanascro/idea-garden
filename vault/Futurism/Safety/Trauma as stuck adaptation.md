## Core idea

Trauma is not permanent damage but an adaptive survival response that remains active after the original threat has passed.

## Structure

The nervous system evolves to detect danger and mobilize protective responses such as hypervigilance, avoidance, or emotional numbing. These responses are functional during real threat. Trauma arises when those protective mechanisms remain activated long after safety has returned. Healing therefore does not require repairing a broken person but gently reducing unnecessary survival effort. Support, therapy, and safe environments allow the nervous system to recalibrate, gradually expanding the range of experiences that feel safe again.

## Human signal

The person is not ruined; their system is protecting them more than the present requires.

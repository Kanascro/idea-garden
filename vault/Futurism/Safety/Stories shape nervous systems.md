## Core idea

The narratives a culture repeats train nervous systems, which in turn shape behavior and social norms.

## Structure

Stories function as rehearsal environments for interpreting reality. When narratives repeatedly emphasize danger, betrayal, and punishment, people become primed for suspicion and defensive reactions. When stories emphasize cooperation, repair, and mutual support, they cultivate trust and relational competence. Over time these narrative patterns influence how communities interpret everyday interactions. Cultural storytelling therefore becomes a powerful mechanism for shaping collective behavior. By changing the stories societies tell about conflict and human nature, it becomes possible to reshape how people respond to one another.

## Human signal

A cooperative society begins with cooperative imagination.

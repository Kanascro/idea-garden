## Core idea

The purpose of justice is not punishment but reducing the probability of future harm by expanding the capacities of individuals, communities, and environments.

## Structure

Punitive justice treats wrongdoing as debt that must be paid through suffering or exclusion. A preventive model instead asks what conditions produced the event and what changes would prevent similar outcomes. Accountability becomes the process of learning, skill development, environmental redesign, and relational repair. Systems focus on building communication skills, strengthening boundaries, reshaping risky contexts, and developing cultural norms that reduce misunderstanding and coercion. Justice becomes forward-oriented: its success is measured by whether harm becomes less likely over time.

## Human signal

Accountability remains real, but its purpose becomes growth rather than destruction.

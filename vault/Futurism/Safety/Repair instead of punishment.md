## Core idea

Conflict and harm can become opportunities for relational repair and skill-building rather than triggers for shame, exile, or revenge.

## Structure

In a repair-oriented system, the goal is to restore safety, acknowledge impact, and rebuild functioning relationships when possible. Structured mediation, needs negotiation, and trauma-informed listening allow individuals to understand what happened, what signals were missed, and what new agreements can prevent repetition. Repair does not mean the absence of consequences. Instead, consequences take the form of learning processes, boundary changes, and support structures that enable healthier behavior moving forward.

## Human signal

Conflict becomes something communities know how to metabolize rather than something that permanently fractures them.

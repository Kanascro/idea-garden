## Core idea

Information can be presented as personal understanding rather than authoritative advice, allowing individuals to form their own conclusions.

## Structure

A non-prescriptive communication style emphasizes phrases such as “this is how it appears from this perspective” rather than definitive instructions about what others should believe or do. This approach avoids demonizing or glorifying particular choices. Instead, it offers frameworks, observations, and reflections that readers can examine within their own contexts. By removing pressure to conform to a viewpoint, the audience retains intellectual autonomy while still benefiting from shared insight.

## Human signal

People engage more openly with ideas when they feel free to accept, reject, or reinterpret them.

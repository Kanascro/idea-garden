## Core idea

Educational groupings can be organized around competence, understanding, and cognitive style rather than age.

## Structure

Age-based education assumes that individuals learn at similar rates and through similar methods. Competence-based systems instead group learners according to their understanding, interests, and cognitive patterns. This structure accommodates neurodiverse learning styles by allowing multiple ways of approaching problems, interpreting questions, and demonstrating mastery. Progress becomes fluid, enabling individuals to move through topics according to readiness rather than chronological stage.

## Human signal

Learning environments become more inclusive when they adapt to diverse ways of thinking.

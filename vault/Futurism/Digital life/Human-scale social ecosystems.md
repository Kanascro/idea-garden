## Core idea

Social environments function best at human-scale sizes where recognition and trust can form.

## Structure

Instead of offering a global pool of endless novelty, communities operate within bounded membership groups. Participation unfolds gradually, allowing people to become familiar with one another over time. This structure mirrors the social dynamics of villages, campuses, and cooperative spaces where people repeatedly encounter the same individuals.

## Human signal

When the social field is finite, people stop feeling disposable.

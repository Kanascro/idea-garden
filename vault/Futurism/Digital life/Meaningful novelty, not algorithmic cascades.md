## Core idea

The nervous system described here does not resist novelty itself. It resists novelty that arrives as arbitrary velocity, casino logic, or engagement-maximized drift. What it wants is novelty with semantic coherence.

## Structure

Instead of algorithmic cascades that deliver one unrelated stimulus after another, this philosophy supports adjacency: one meaningful object leading to another through relevance, resonance, or conceptual kinship. Discovery should feel like semantic constellation-building rather than surrender to an infinite river of content. The movement from one thing to the next should preserve internal logic. Novelty should arise from relation, not from churn. This means systems should curate by concept, motif, mood, or idea-cluster rather than by whatever sustains maximum continuation. The experience of finding something new should resemble following a trail through a forest, where each turn grows out of the terrain already underfoot, rather than being pushed down a waterslide whose direction is hidden and whose momentum belongs to the system.

## Human signal

The desired feeling is wonder without disorientation. Newness should feel like deepening into a world, not being flung through one.

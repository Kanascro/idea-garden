## Core idea

The user needs digital environments that behave like places rather than streams. A room supports relation, presence, and atmosphere; a feed supports consumption, drift, and extraction.

## Structure

Feeds are conveyor belts. They organize experience as perpetual intake. Rooms are structured spaces with thresholds, corners, centers, atmospheres, and a felt distinction between entry and exit. This philosophy therefore imagines digital spaces as rooms: sites where the user arrives, inhabits, relates, and leaves, rather than endlessly receiving input. A room can hold a mood, a theme, a collection, or a question. It gives spatial and emotional coherence to what is being encountered. Instead of a feed that makes the user consume, a room makes the user situate. Presence replaces throughput. The architecture of room-like digital space would support contemplation, selection, arrival, and a sense of “here I am, with this thing.” It would encourage finite sessions, differentiated atmospheres, and relational modes of attention rather than continuous extraction.

## Human signal

A room lets a person feel located. It replaces the anxiety of being carried along with the steadier feeling of being somewhere.

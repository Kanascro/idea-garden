## Core idea

Humane technology may require aligning digital systems with the rhythms of biological cognition.

## Structure

Human attention and memory operate through cycles of intake, consolidation, resurfacing, and forgetting. When digital systems ignore these rhythms and instead prioritize endless storage and access, they create cognitive pressure. Designing technology that respects these rhythms could restore balance between human minds and digital environments.

## Human signal

Technology can either amplify overload or move in step with the nervous system.

## Core idea

Depth should remain available but never coercive. A sensitive user needs to be able to stop early, go deeper only when it feels right, and remain in relationship to the experience without being pulled into compulsion.

## Structure

Many current systems are designed so that exploration becomes difficult to stop once begun. Each layer opens onto another, and depth becomes a trapdoor rather than a gift. This philosophy treats depth as invitation rather than pressure. A user can remain at the surface, enter one layer, or descend further without being punished for stopping. The architecture should support partial engagement as a valid endpoint. There should be no hidden social or algorithmic pressure that implies failure if the user does not continue. This restores agency to curiosity. A stream can be approached like a stream one sits beside, not one falls into. Systems should communicate that enough can happen at any layer of engagement, and that closure is always available.

## Human signal

This feels like trust. The platform does not panic when you leave. It does not convert your curiosity into leverage against you.

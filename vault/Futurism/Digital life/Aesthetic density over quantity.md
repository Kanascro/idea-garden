## Core idea

The system should prioritize concentration, intensity, and coherence rather than volume. The user might not want thousands of weak inputs. The user might want a few things that land, deepen, and belong together.

## Structure

Contemporary platforms often assume more is inherently richer, but this nervous system experiences quantity as dilution once it passes a certain threshold. Ten thousand mediocre images produce noise, not nourishment. What matters is aesthetic density: one thing that hits, three things that deepen it, ten things that genuinely form a set. A humane platform would optimize for meaningful concentration rather than maximal saving, maximal browsing, or maximal archive size. It might even impose small daily caps not as punishment but as a way to restore perceptual weight to each saved item. This changes the user’s relationship to curation. Saving becomes an act of discernment rather than hoarding. Accumulation gives way to composition. The system supports the formation of small, resonant clusters instead of sprawling masses of low-salience material.

## Human signal

This is less like stockpiling and more like making an altar, a shelf, or a bouquet. The point is not to have more. The point is to feel what is there.

## Core idea

The goal is not maximal ease. It is matching the texture of effort to the emotional or cognitive weight of an action so that interaction remains intentional rather than frictionless in the predatory sense.

## Structure

This philosophy rejects the assumption that lower friction is always better. A Pinterest link opening in a browser rather than the app creates light friction that protects the user from reflexive immersion. “Next page” instead of infinite scroll acts as a soft brake, not a prohibition. A visible folder capacity creates a threshold that makes accumulation legible. One tap to save versus three taps to save becomes a meaningful design question rather than a trivial efficiency metric. The user does not want arbitrary restrictions. The user wants tactile pace. Humans benefit when effort has texture and when actions feel proportionate to their consequences. Efficiency belongs to machine logic. Intentionality often requires soft resistance, enough to keep the person present to what they are doing. Proportional friction is therefore not punitive; it is a way of preserving contact between desire, choice, and action.

## Human signal

This is the feeling of being gently accompanied rather than accelerated. The system says: you can do this, but you do not need to disappear into doing it.

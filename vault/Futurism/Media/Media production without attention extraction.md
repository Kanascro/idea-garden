## Core idea

Cultural infrastructure must be stabilized outside market pressures while markets remain spaces for creative variation that cannot manipulate psychological vulnerabilities.

## Structure

A society maintains publicly funded broadcasters, archives, libraries, and creator grant systems that guarantee baseline cultural production and access. Artists receive support through peer review, audience contribution pools, and public cultural institutions. Alongside this core, independent studios experiment with niche formats, subscription projects, and limited creative releases. However, systems that maximize engagement through manipulation are prohibited: no surveillance-based advertising, no algorithmic outrage amplification, and no design optimized for addictive infinite scrolling. Platforms refuse distribution to producers attempting to monetize polarization or psychological dependency.

## Human signal

Markets create diversity of voices and forms without turning human attention into an extractable resource.

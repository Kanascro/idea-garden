## Core idea

Housing systems can remove landlord–tenant asymmetry by decommodifying land and embedding collective ownership or public provision.

## Structure

Public housing treats shelter as infrastructure, ensuring stable, non-speculative access. Community land trusts separate land ownership from markets, preserving affordability through collective control and resale limits. Cooperative housing replaces hierarchical ownership with democratic governance, making residents both users and decision-makers. These models shift housing from asset extraction to shared stewardship.

## Human signal

The issue is not just fairness within the system, but the structure of the system itself. Shelter becomes something held in common rather than controlled.

## Core idea

Consent acknowledges that each person has the right to determine what happens to their body, time, and personal choices.

## Structure

Many social norms historically assumed access to others’ boundaries, whether through unsolicited advice, expected physical affection, or pressure to respond to questions. A consent-based framework challenges these assumptions by recognizing autonomy as the default state. Participation in any interaction becomes voluntary rather than socially required. This perspective transforms everyday interactions into acts of negotiated respect rather than habitual intrusion.

## Human signal

Autonomy is preserved when a person’s “yes” is freely given and their “no” is accepted without resistance.

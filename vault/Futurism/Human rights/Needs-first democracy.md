## Core idea

Democratic problem-solving can begin with identifying human needs before debating solutions.

## Structure

Drawing from Nonviolent Communication, participants first surface the underlying needs present in a situation—security, autonomy, belonging, stability, fairness, care. Once the needs are visible, people co-create solutions designed to meet as many of those needs as possible. The focus shifts from defending positions to collaboratively designing responses that address shared human requirements.

## Human signal

The tone of the process changes: instead of arguing over preferred answers, people become curious about what each person is trying to protect or nourish.

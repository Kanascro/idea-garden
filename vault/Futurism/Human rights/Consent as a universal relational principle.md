## Core idea

Consent is not limited to sexuality; it is a foundational principle governing how humans interact with each other’s autonomy, boundaries, and inner worlds.

## Structure

Consent operates wherever one person’s actions affect another’s body, attention, or life direction. This includes physical touch, emotional engagement, advice, and even the expectation of answers. Recognizing consent as universal shifts relationships away from assumption and toward mutual agreement. It requires awareness that every person has sovereignty over their space, body, and decisions. Rather than acting automatically, individuals learn to check whether participation is welcomed before entering another person’s experience.

## Human signal

Respect becomes visible when interaction begins with invitation rather than assumption.

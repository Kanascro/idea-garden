## Core idea

Anti-hierarchical systems do not require ranking people as better or worse; they require boundaries that prevent domination. The goal is not a ladder of authority but a structural fence around unacceptable concentrations of power.

## Structure

Instead of policing behavior moment-to-moment, systems can pre-commit to constraints that shape how power flows. Examples include exit rights, rotating coordination roles, public reasoning behind decisions, limits on unilateral control of money or resources, independent appeal pathways, automatic time limits on roles, and decentralized access to knowledge and tools. These are not moral judgments about individuals. They are structural conditions that prevent temporary influence from hardening into durable control.

## Human signal

The spirit is protective rather than punitive: keep emergence alive while quietly blocking the pathways through which hierarchy tends to crystallize.

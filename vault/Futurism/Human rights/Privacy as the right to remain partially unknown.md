## Core idea

Privacy is not just control over data, but the ability to remain opaque within a system.

## Structure

Even with consent, systems that continuously observe and interpret behavior can create pressure to become legible, predictable, and optimized. This shifts participation from voluntary expression to subtle self-surveillance. A robust design must therefore protect not only data ownership, but the right to not be fully interpreted or exposed.

## Human signal

There is a quiet resistance here: a refusal to be fully seen on someone else’s terms.

## Core idea

Needs-based frameworks interpret human behavior through underlying motivations rather than moral categories of right and wrong.

## Structure

Approaches such as needs-centered communication emphasize that actions often emerge from attempts to meet universal human needs—security, connection, autonomy, recognition, or belonging. When interactions are analyzed through these motivations, conflict becomes a signal of unmet needs rather than evidence of personal failure or moral deficiency. This perspective reduces adversarial thinking and encourages curiosity about underlying dynamics in social and interpersonal systems.

## Human signal

Empathy increases when behavior is viewed as an attempt to meet needs rather than as a moral transgression.

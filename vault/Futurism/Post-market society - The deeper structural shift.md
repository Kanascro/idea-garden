## Core idea

A stable post-market society requires two linked transformations: status attaches to contribution rather than accumulation, and power attaches to responsibility rather than wealth.

## Structure

If wealth no longer confers automatic authority, individuals cannot convert financial success into social dominance. Governance, cultural legitimacy, and moral authority arise from collective institutions and demonstrated stewardship rather than purchasing power. Markets remain functional subsystems performing technical roles—testing ideas, allocating suppliers, and refining tools—without determining the social hierarchy. Ambition therefore continues, but its pathways shift toward excellence, responsibility, and service.

## Human signal

Ambition does not disappear. It becomes directional rather than extractive.

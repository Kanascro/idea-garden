## Core idea

Food distribution systems can prioritize unprocessed nutrition by restricting industrially processed foods from mainstream retail environments.

## Structure

Highly processed foods often dominate commercial food systems due to cost efficiency and long shelf life. A regulatory approach that restricts such products encourages the availability of whole foods and minimally processed ingredients. This shift reframes food policy as a public health intervention aimed at improving long-term physical and cognitive well-being. Regulation functions not as prohibition but as structural guidance toward healthier dietary norms.

## Human signal

Food becomes nourishment rather than engineered consumption.

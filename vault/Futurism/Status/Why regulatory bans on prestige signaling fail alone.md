## Core idea

Rules restricting logos or advertising cannot succeed if underlying prestige structures still reward wealth accumulation.

## Structure

When wealth remains the main indicator of power and credibility, individuals continuously invent new signals of status. These signals may be subtle aesthetics, insider cultural markers, or coded consumption patterns. Even if explicit branding disappears, elites create new forms of symbolic distinction. Capitalism succeeded historically not by inventing status hierarchies but by converting them into scalable monetary proxies. Therefore a society that merely restricts marketing while leaving wealth as the dominant prestige signal inevitably reproduces inequality through alternative markers.

## Human signal

Social systems decay when prestige incentives contradict official rules. Cultural admiration must shift before regulatory boundaries can hold.

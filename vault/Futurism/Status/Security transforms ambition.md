Human motivation changes dramatically when survival and dignity are guaranteed.

## Structure

Under conditions of scarcity and insecurity, individuals prioritize domination and resource control because survival depends on it. Psychological research and anthropological evidence suggest that once basic security is established, motivations diversify. People seek differentiation through mastery, recognition, and meaningful contribution rather than simple accumulation. This shift appears in childhood development, post-war reconstruction societies, and experiments providing universal security guarantees. The central transformation is not the disappearance of ambition but its redirection toward excellence rather than dominance.

## Human signal

When survival anxiety fades, admiration from peers becomes more compelling than control over others.

## Core idea

Basic material dignity is guaranteed outside the market, while markets remain spaces for aesthetic experimentation rather than status signaling.

## Structure

A public clothing commons provides durable, repairable garments designed for comfort and function. Multiple cuts, fabrics, and sensory-friendly options exist to accommodate diverse bodies and needs. These garments carry no logos or prestige markers, and access to them does not influence employment, credibility, or social inclusion. Beyond the commons, markets exist for expressive clothing: experimental designs, commissioned pieces, and joyful aesthetic exploration. However, advertising cannot associate clothing with intelligence, virtue, success, or social rank, and institutions cannot enforce status-based dress codes. Clothing markets therefore serve creativity and play rather than hierarchy formation.

## Human signal

A bright, eccentric hat can exist without granting symbolic power. Aesthetic expression survives, but identity and dignity no longer depend on it.

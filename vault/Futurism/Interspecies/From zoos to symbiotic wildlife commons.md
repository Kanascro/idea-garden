## Core idea

Animal spaces could shift from ownership-based captivity to ecosystems where animals participate voluntarily.

## Structure

Traditional zoos operate on possession: animals are bred, confined, and displayed. A symbiotic model would instead design environments that attract animals naturally through habitat quality, safety, and mutual benefit. Animals would be free to come and go, while humans create conditions—food sources, shelter, and ecological niches—that make the space attractive without enforcing containment.

## Human signal

The relationship shifts from possession to coexistence.

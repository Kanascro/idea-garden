## Core idea

A society may redefine relationships with animals by rejecting ownership and treating animals as autonomous beings rather than property.

## Structure

Traditional pet ownership often assumes that animals can be possessed, confined, and bred for human companionship. A rights-centered framework reframes this relationship as a form of domination, arguing that animals should not exist primarily as dependent objects within human households. Instead, animals would live within protected ecological systems where their autonomy and natural behaviors are prioritized. Human interaction would shift toward stewardship of habitats rather than private ownership.

## Human signal

Compassion extends beyond affection toward animals and becomes respect for their independence.

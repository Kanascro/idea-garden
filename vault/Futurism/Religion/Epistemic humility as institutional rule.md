## Core idea

The Meaning Commons avoids becoming a new secular orthodoxy by making humility structural rather than optional.

## Structure

The danger in any institution devoted to meaning is that it can harden around preferred interpretations and begin treating dissent as threat. To prevent this, humility cannot be left as a personality trait of leaders. It has to be codified into format. Every framework taught must be accompanied by a “where it fails” component. Every discussion culture must treat critique as participation rather than sabotage. No school of thought gets to present itself as immune to distortion, historical misuse, or contextual limitation. This protects against the common secular failure mode where an anti-dogmatic space quietly develops its own unquestionable assumptions.

The result is a distinctive epistemic culture. People are invited into seriousness without being asked for submission. Intellectual life is restored as a public practice rather than a prestige game. Participants learn not only frameworks but a habit of handling frameworks lightly: to use them, compare them, test them, and release them when they overreach. That is very different from relativism. It is disciplined pluralism.

## Human signal

The emotional effect is relief. One can think deeply without joining an ideological army.

## Core idea

A non-religious replacement should be built as public commons rather than belief community. Its legitimacy comes from service, accessibility, and shared governance, not from claims to final truth.

## Structure

The crucial institutional move is to separate function from doctrine. Belonging should not depend on assent. Care should not require identity conformity. Ritual should not imply metaphysical submission. Meaning should be explored through plural frameworks rather than fixed orthodoxy. This means the replacement institution must be funded and structured like civic infrastructure, closer to libraries, parks, clinics, and schools than to churches or brands. A commons model changes the incentive landscape: participants are not customers to retain, disciples to discipline, or souls to save. They are residents, neighbors, and co-stewards of a shared social resource. The point is not to create a secular church with softer language. The point is to create infrastructure that remains useful even when people disagree about reality.

## Human signal

The emotional tone here is one of shelter without enclosure. A person should be able to enter, receive something real, and leave without feeling owned.

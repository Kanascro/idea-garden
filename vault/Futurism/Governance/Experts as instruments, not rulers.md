## Core idea

Experts may be necessary for discussing means, but their role must be designed so expertise informs decisions without becoming another channel of capture.

## Structure

Scientific or technical questions require domain knowledge that general voting cannot replace. But once experts are inserted into the system, the same risks return: lobbying, institutional capture, status games, and hidden ideology. Expertise solves one problem while creating another, so the governance of experts becomes part of the political architecture.

## Human signal

There is trust in knowledge here, but not innocence about the structures that surround people who hold it.

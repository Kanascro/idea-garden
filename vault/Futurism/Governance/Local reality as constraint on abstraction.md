## Core idea

Material conditions ultimately limit how far political abstraction can go.

## Structure

Some decisions—food systems, land use, resource flows—are inseparable from geography. Attempts to universalize them risk becoming detached from actual conditions. A grounded system reintroduces locality at the point of execution, ensuring that decisions remain responsive to specific environments and capacities.

## Human signal

There is a quiet return to humility: not everything can be decided from everywhere.

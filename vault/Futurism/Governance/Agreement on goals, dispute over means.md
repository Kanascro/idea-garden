## Core idea

Political process may work better if collective goals are identified first and methods are debated second.

## Structure

Many conflicts fuse together values, goals, and implementation, making disagreement feel total. Separating them could reduce noise: people might share ends while differing on mechanisms. Scientific knowledge or domain expertise would then become more useful at the level of means rather than as a substitute for public values.

## Human signal

There is a hope that politics could become less tribal if people discovered they want similar things before arguing about how to get there.

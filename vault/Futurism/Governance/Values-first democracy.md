## Core idea

Collective decision-making can begin with mapping shared goals rather than debating predefined policies.

## Structure

Instead of voting on packaged solutions, individuals express priorities, trade-offs, and values directly. Aggregating these signals reveals patterns of alignment, making it possible to identify common goals before discussing implementation. This separates what people want from how to achieve it, reducing ideological bundling.

## Human signal

There is a desire to be understood more precisely than current systems allow.

## Core idea

Collective coordination does not need to be organized around inherited nationality or fixed territorial belonging.

## Structure

People can align politically through shared priorities rather than birthplace or state identity. This allows governance to become more adaptive, plural, and experimental. Material systems like water, roads, and power still require geographic coordination, but cultural and political affiliation do not need to follow the same boundaries.

## Human signal

Belonging becomes less about where you were assigned and more about what kind of world you are trying to help build.

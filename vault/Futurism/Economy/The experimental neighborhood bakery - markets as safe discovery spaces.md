## Core idea

Markets are appropriate for domains where experimentation is culturally interesting but failure carries minimal systemic harm.

## Structure

In a neighborhood where staple food security is already guaranteed through public systems, independent bakeries can operate as experimental ventures. Different organizational models coexist: a worker cooperative, a fermentation-focused craft lab, and a small profit-seeking bakery exploring hyperlocal grain sourcing. The market here functions as a discovery process rather than a survival filter. It reveals which recipes people value, which labor models sustain workers, and which practices produce community attachment. Over time, some ventures fail, others transform, and successful innovations may migrate into the municipal food system. The market thus serves as a sandbox generating ideas that can later be stabilized outside it.

## Human signal

The presence of risk remains playful rather than existential. Bakers test creativity without the fear that failure will deprive anyone of food or dignity.

## Core idea

Digital infrastructure remains open and publicly governed while markets support modular improvements that compete on craft and utility.

## Structure

Core software platforms used for public planning or governance remain open-source and governed by transparent technical councils. The system architecture ensures that data, algorithms, and decision processes remain auditable. Within this framework, private teams can develop plug-ins, visualization tools, accessibility modules, and specialized analytics packages. Competition centers on usability, technical elegance, and integration quality. However, vendors cannot lock user data, obscure algorithms, or monetize behavioral information. Successful modules spread organically through adoption, while weak ones disappear. The market thus supports incremental innovation without enclosing the underlying infrastructure.

## Human signal

Developers compete as craftspeople refining tools rather than gatekeepers controlling digital territory.

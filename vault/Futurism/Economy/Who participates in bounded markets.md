## Core idea

When markets are constrained and cannot generate domination, they attract participants motivated by craft, curiosity, and system optimization rather than power.

## Structure

Individuals who thrive in such environments tend to be craft competitors who enjoy improving designs and benchmarking skill, playful optimizers who treat systems as iterative games, and transitional stewards who understand markets as temporary mechanisms supporting broader public goals. Because these markets cannot accumulate vast power or shape social hierarchies, empire-building personalities find them less appealing. Instead, they become arenas for technical excellence, experimentation, and cooperative competition.

## Human signal

Markets stop being ladders to domination and become specialized environments where people test ideas and refine skills.

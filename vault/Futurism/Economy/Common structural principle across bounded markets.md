## Core idea

Across domains, markets function safely only when purpose and standards are defined outside them.

## Structure

In each example, the social objective—care, food security, dignity, safety, cultural integrity, or digital transparency—is established through governance before any market activity begins. Markets operate inside those boundaries as technical allocation systems or creative sandboxes. Participation in or exit from the market never determines a person’s access to dignity or survival. Marketing is constrained so that it communicates function rather than manipulating identity or fear. Failure is permitted only where consequences remain contained and reversible. The market never determines who deserves resources, credibility, or belonging.

## Human signal

Markets become instruments rather than authorities. They are tools societies use deliberately, not forces that shape moral reality.

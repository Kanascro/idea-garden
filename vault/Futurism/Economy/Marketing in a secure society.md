## Core idea

When status and survival are decoupled from consumption, marketing transforms from psychological manipulation into informational signaling.

## Structure

In insecure societies, marketing exploits feelings of inadequacy, urgency, and social comparison. Advertisements promise identity upgrades and social legitimacy through consumption. In a secure environment where dignity is guaranteed, these emotional levers lose potency. Marketing therefore shifts toward describing function, purpose, and suitability: what a product does, why it exists, and who might appreciate it. Identity claims and moral insinuations disappear because they no longer produce reliable behavioral responses.

## Human signal

Communication becomes invitation rather than pressure.

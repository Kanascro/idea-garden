## Core idea

Rule-breaking persists in any system, but its cultural and economic rewards determine whether it spreads.

## Structure

In systems where manipulation leads to enormous wealth or power, opportunists who exploit loopholes become dominant actors and role models. When markets are bounded and cannot generate dominance, the rewards for cheating shrink dramatically. Exposure damages reputation rather than merely financial standing, and the absence of large jackpots discourages systematic exploitation. Rule-breakers still appear, but they fail to attract widespread imitation or institutional capture.

## Human signal

Predatory behavior loses glamour when it stops producing winners.

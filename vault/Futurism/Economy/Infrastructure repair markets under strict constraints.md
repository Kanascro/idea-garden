## Core idea

Even in technically complex public works, markets can function as constrained contractors operating within non-negotiable public standards.

## Structure

When a bridge requires repair, engineers first establish fixed performance requirements such as structural resilience, lifespan expectations exceeding fifty years, and rigorous safety margins. These standards define the acceptable design space before any bidding occurs. Certified engineering firms then compete within this constrained environment. Contracts reward long-term durability, transparency of materials and processes, and designs that simplify future maintenance. All bids and results are publicly visible, and firms that compromise safety face cumulative penalties and loss of eligibility across the entire public sector. Market competition therefore drives technical excellence while remaining subordinate to public safety goals.

## Human signal

The market becomes disciplined and cautious. Firms compete intensely but cannot externalize failure onto society.

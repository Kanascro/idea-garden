## Core idea

Markets are effective at rapid experimentation and failure selection, but incapable of stewarding essentials. A stable society confines markets to bounded zones where failure is tolerable and consequences are contained.

## Structure

Markets excel at decentralized testing: many actors try variations, weak approaches disappear quickly, and useful ones spread. This evolutionary property becomes destructive when applied to systems where failure harms dignity, safety, or social trust. Therefore markets must operate only where experimentation is safe and reversible. They become provisional tools: time-limited, reviewable, and governed by external standards. When an activity becomes essential, formative, or system-critical, it exits the market layer and moves under collective governance. The governing principle is not ideology but risk containment: markets may explore the edges, but they cannot define the foundations.

## Human signal

This reverses the modern pattern where everything begins sacred and ends monetized. Here, experimentation is tolerated at the margins while the core of social life remains protected from commodification.

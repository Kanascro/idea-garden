## Core idea

Green spaces can be designed as biodiverse ecosystems rather than decorative landscaping.

## Structure

Many urban parks are maintained as controlled lawns with limited ecological diversity. A biodiversity-oriented approach treats green spaces as living ecosystems that support native species, varied plant life, and ecological resilience. Instead of ornamental greenery, these spaces function as habitats, pollinator corridors, and climate stabilizers. Urban planning integrates ecological health as a foundational component of city design rather than as aesthetic decoration.

## Human signal

Nature becomes something alive and complex within the city rather than a simplified backdrop.

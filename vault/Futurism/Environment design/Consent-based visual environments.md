## Core idea

Public environments can be designed to minimize visual overload by allowing individuals to control the amount of sensory information they encounter.

## Structure

Urban spaces often contain advertising, signage, and visual clutter that individuals cannot easily avoid. A consent-based design framework reduces permanent visual noise and shifts optional visual layers into augmented reality systems. Information, decoration, or commercial content becomes filterable according to personal preferences. This approach supports neurodiverse perception and reduces cognitive fatigue by allowing individuals to regulate the complexity of their visual surroundings.

## Human signal

Cities become calmer and more navigable when sensory input is adjustable rather than imposed.

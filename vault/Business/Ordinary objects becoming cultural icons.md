## Core idea

Cultural icons often begin as ordinary tools that quietly solve everyday problems better than alternatives.

## Structure

Iconic status rarely emerges from attempts to appear exceptional. Instead, it grows from long-term presence in practical situations where people repeatedly rely on the same solution. Consistency of design, accessibility of price or format, and integration into daily life create familiarity. Over time, this familiarity transforms into cultural symbolism. What began as a simple tool becomes a recognizable artifact associated with a particular stage of life or shared experience.

## Human signal

The object carries emotional resonance because it has accompanied people through meaningful moments.

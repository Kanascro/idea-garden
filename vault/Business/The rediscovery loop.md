## Core idea

Long-lasting cultural products cycle through periods of mass adoption and rediscovery across generations.

## Structure

The lifecycle often follows a pattern: initial usefulness within a niche community, expansion through widespread exposure, normalization through uniform adoption, followed by a period where the product becomes culturally invisible. After several years, a new generation rediscovers the tool and reinterprets it as fresh or meaningful again. This cycle can be supported intentionally through consistent visual identity, stable design, and entry points for younger communities or emerging cultural groups. Longevity rather than constant reinvention allows the product to accumulate cultural memory.

## Human signal

The product feels timeless rather than trendy, carrying familiarity across generations.

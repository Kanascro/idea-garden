## Core idea

Products that aim to become defaults must remain socially safe, visually recognizable, and operationally simple.

## Structure

When a tool is perceived as too ideological, niche, or stylistically extreme, potential adopters hesitate due to social risk. Defaults succeed when they occupy a neutral cultural position—accessible to diverse users and adaptable to different contexts. Clear language, recognizable formats, and simple design reduce hesitation. The product’s reliability becomes its defining trait: people trust it because it works consistently and does not demand identity alignment.

## Human signal

The tool feels easy to introduce in conversation, therapy, or everyday life without embarrassment or explanation.

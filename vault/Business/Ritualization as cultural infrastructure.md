## Core idea

Tools become durable cultural institutions when they evolve into simple rituals that people repeat across relationships and generations.

## Structure

A ritual transforms occasional use into habitual practice. For relational tools, this might include structured check-in conversations, shared agreement reviews, reflection nights, or periodic emotional audits. Once a ritual format becomes widely recognizable, distribution occurs organically: partners introduce it to new partners, therapists introduce it to clients, and communities normalize it as standard practice. The key is simplicity and repeatability. A ritual must be easy enough to perform without instruction and meaningful enough that people want to return to it.

## Human signal

The activity becomes part of relational culture—something people do together rather than something they buy.

## Core idea

Cultural adoption accelerates when tools are anchored to specific life transitions or recurring relational challenges.

## Structure

Certain moments reliably create uncertainty and openness to guidance. When a product becomes the standard response to these triggers, distribution becomes self-reinforcing. People recommend the tool because it already belongs to the cultural script of that situation. The strategy therefore focuses less on marketing campaigns and more on identifying and occupying these recurring decision points.

## Human signal

The product becomes associated with moments of care, clarity, and growth rather than with advertising.

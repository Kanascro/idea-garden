## Core idea

Religion’s enduring power comes from the fact that it solves real human coordination and existential problems. Its enduring danger comes from solving them through centralized authority over meaning, morality, and belonging.

## Structure

At its best, religion acts as social infrastructure: it gathers people regularly, gives shared language for suffering and responsibility, marks life transitions, organizes care in crisis, and creates ritual forms that calm bodies and synchronize communities. These are not trivial additions to life; they are foundational supports that many secular societies have neglected to replace. At its worst, the same infrastructure becomes a capture device. The institution that names meaning can also define deviance. The community that offers belonging can also threaten exile. The ritual that soothes can become obedience training. The moral language that helps people orient can become a surveillance system for bodies, desire, thought, and dissent. The same architecture can support life or dominate it depending on who controls access, interpretation, and legitimacy.

## Human signal

This is why simple anti-religious dismissal misses the point. People return to religion not only because of belief, but because the underlying human needs never disappeared.

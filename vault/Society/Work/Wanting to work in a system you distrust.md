## Core idea

Difficulty wanting to work can arise when personal values conflict with the dominant economic system, creating a psychological disconnect between survival and meaning.

## Structure

Many people experience resistance to work not because they lack motivation but because the surrounding system frames labor primarily as extraction of value rather than contribution to life. When work is associated with coercion, hierarchy, or profit maximization, individuals who value autonomy, creativity, or care can feel alienated from the activity itself. The tension emerges from needing income for survival while rejecting the ideological framing of the system providing that income. Reframing work as a practical means of sustaining life rather than as moral endorsement of the system can reduce this internal conflict.

## Human signal

Motivation often returns when work is interpreted as supporting personal life rather than serving the system itself.

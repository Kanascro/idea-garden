## Core idea

The desire to work and contribute develops through participation in social environments where effort is visible, guided, and shared.

## Structure

Motivation is often treated as an individual psychological trait, but historically it functioned as a collective behavior learned through imitation and participation. When young people regularly see others building, repairing, teaching, and creating, work becomes a normal extension of life rather than an abstract obligation. Without these visible models, effort can feel arbitrary and disconnected from meaning. Social environments that emphasize collaboration, mentorship, and shared projects help transform motivation from a personal struggle into a learned habit.

## Human signal

Energy emerges more easily when effort is embedded in a community of practice.

## Core idea

Money is a tool for exchange that has been mistaken for the thing it represents.

## Structure

Originally a placeholder to facilitate trade, money became conflated with value itself. This shift transformed qualitative experiences—time, care, attention—into quantified units, redefining worth in numerical terms. The confusion lies not in using money, but in treating it as equivalent to life’s essentials.

## Human signal

There is a quiet disentangling here: a desire to remember what actually sustains a life.

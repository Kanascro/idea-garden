## Core idea

Open play carries social risk because it challenges expectations of control and status.

## Structure

Displaying playfulness can signal a break from seriousness, exposing individuals to being perceived as less competent or credible. Social systems reinforce restraint indirectly, rewarding predictability and discouraging deviation. The taboo persists through collective self-regulation rather than explicit prohibition.

## Human signal

To play openly is to risk being misread.

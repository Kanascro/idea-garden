## Core idea

The desire to play is widespread, but often inhibited by social fear.

## Structure

Play involves spontaneity, embodiment, and emotional openness, yet is constrained by norms of composure and self-presentation. The gap between wanting to play and actually doing it is maintained by fear of judgment, rejection, or loss of credibility. Moments where others express playfulness often reveal this tension as latent desire held in check.

## Human signal

People don’t lack playfulness—they hesitate at the edge of expressing it.

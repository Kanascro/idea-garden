## Core idea

Shared environments subtly dictate which emotions and behaviors are acceptable.

## Structure

Norms of neutrality, efficiency, and predictability shape how individuals behave in collective settings. When an unexpected expression of play appears, it disrupts these norms and momentarily expands what feels permissible. This reveals that emotional expression is not fixed, but contingent on what is modeled and tolerated.

## Human signal

A small shift in behavior can unlock a wider range of feeling for everyone present.

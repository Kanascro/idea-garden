## Core idea

In life, multiple choices are legitimate because each reflects a different valid need or value.

## Structure

Decisions are often framed as right versus wrong, yet many life choices simply express different priorities—self-protection, growth, curiosity, commitment, or rest. When several values are in tension, each possible direction holds its own integrity depending on what a person is ready for or needs most.

## Human signal

Relief can arise when the pressure to find the single “correct” answer softens.

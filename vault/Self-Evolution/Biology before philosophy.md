## Core idea

Before interpreting emotional states as existential truths, basic biological needs should be checked first.

## Structure

Fatigue, hunger, dehydration, inactivity, overstimulation, and hormonal cycles can strongly affect mood and perception. When these physiological variables are unbalanced, the mind may generate sweeping conclusions about life, relationships, or identity that actually reflect temporary bodily states rather than stable insights.

## Human signal

Sometimes the most profound intervention is the least dramatic: eat, sleep, move, and step outside.

## Core idea

A sense of awe and relational wonder functions as a psychological nutrient rather than a rare emotional luxury.

## Structure

Experiences of beauty, nature, and meaningful connection activate states of openness, humility, and belonging that nourish psychological well-being. When these experiences are absent, life can feel mechanistic and emotionally flat. Environments that allow contact with beauty, living systems, and genuine human presence sustain the capacity for curiosity and care. Relationships and places that evoke wonder therefore serve a stabilizing and energizing role in a person’s life.

## Human signal

Awe renews attention and restores the sense that life is larger than routine survival.

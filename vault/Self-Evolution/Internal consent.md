## Core idea

The concept of consent can apply within the self: actions align best when internal willingness is present rather than when behavior is driven by pressure or negotiation.

## Structure

Internal consent describes the state in which an action arises from genuine readiness rather than from a rule imposed by self-discipline or emotional urgency. When behavior overrides hesitation or reluctance in order to achieve a psychological outcome, the relationship between intention and experience becomes strained. Respecting internal consent preserves coherence between desire, action, and emotional response. This coherence strengthens the reliability of internal signals over time.

## Human signal

Alignment is experienced when willingness precedes action rather than being forced after it.

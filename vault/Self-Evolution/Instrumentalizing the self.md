## Core idea

A subtle form of self-misalignment occurs when actions are performed primarily to manufacture a desired emotional outcome rather than to express genuine inclination.

## Structure

When behaviors are chosen as strategies to produce feelings—such as calm, independence, or healing—the action becomes instrumental rather than experiential. The activity is no longer pursued for its intrinsic value but as a mechanism intended to change internal state. This creates an internal managerial dynamic where one part of the self attempts to direct or correct another. Over time, this can weaken trust in authentic impulses because the activity is experienced as obligation rather than expression.

## Human signal

The internal tone shifts from curiosity toward quiet resistance when experience is treated as a tool rather than a choice.

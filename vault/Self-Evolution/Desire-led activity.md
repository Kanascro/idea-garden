## Core idea

Activities intended to nourish well-being function most effectively when they originate from genuine attraction or curiosity rather than corrective intention.

## Structure

Practices such as solitude, reflection, creativity, or exploration often lose their restorative qualities when framed as obligations designed to repair emotional states. When the same activities arise from spontaneous interest, they engage motivational systems associated with openness and reward. The shift from corrective behavior to desire-led engagement transforms the activity from self-management into lived experience.

## Human signal

Interest produces energy, whereas obligation produces subtle tension.

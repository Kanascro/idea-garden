## Core idea

Creative and intellectual life grows better from seeds—small beginnings allowed to evolve—than from rigid systems that demand commitment.

## Structure

Structures tend to enforce loyalty and stability, often freezing ideas before they mature. Seeds operate differently: they require time, patience, and openness to transformation. This orientation privileges exploration over control and allows ideas to develop through interaction with the world rather than strict design.

## Human signal

The preference for seeds reflects trust in organic growth. It values surprise, emergence, and the quiet intelligence of time.

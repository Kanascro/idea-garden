## Core idea

Treating current social practices as historical phenomena reveals that what feels normal today is contingent and temporary.

## Structure

Every society organizes life through institutions, habits, and norms that appear natural to the people living within them. When those same practices are examined from an outside or future perspective, they become visible as cultural constructions shaped by specific historical conditions.

## Human signal

What once felt inevitable becomes curious once it is seen as just one way people once lived.

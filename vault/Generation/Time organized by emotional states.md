## Core idea

A calendar can be structured around desired emotional experiences rather than predefined tasks.

## Structure

Instead of scheduling actions, blocks of time are dedicated to feelings—calm, curiosity, connection, play, focus. Activities then emerge as ways to access those states rather than as obligations. This reverses the usual logic: tasks no longer dictate experience; experience guides what is done.

## Human signal

Time feels less like something to manage and more like something to inhabit.

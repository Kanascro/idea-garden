## Core idea

A to-do list is not a set of tasks but a fragmented map of multiple competing realities trying to materialize.

## Structure

Each item represents a different world with its own logic and desire. Boredom emerges when these worlds are flattened into a single system of completion and control. Reframing tasks as portals restores dimensionality and reveals underlying tensions between creation, maintenance, and meaning-making. The list becomes navigable when treated as a landscape rather than a queue.

## Human signal

The shift replaces dull obligation with curiosity. The list stops being oppressive and starts feeling alive, unstable, and slightly magical.

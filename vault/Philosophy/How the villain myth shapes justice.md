## Core idea

When harm is framed through villain narratives, punishment becomes easier than understanding.

## Structure

The myth justifies violence, encourages retribution, and suppresses curiosity about underlying causes. If a person is defined as a villain, their actions appear to originate from inherent evil rather than conditions, histories, or systems. This framing discourages restorative responses and reinforces punitive ones.

## Human signal

Once someone is cast as the monster, listening feels unnecessary.

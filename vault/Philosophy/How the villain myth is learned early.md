## Core idea

The idea of the villain is installed early through the narrative structure of childhood stories.

## Structure

Fairy tales, cartoons, and moral fables introduce the world as a struggle between “good guys” and “bad guys.” This binary becomes early cognitive scaffolding that teaches children to interpret conflict through moral archetypes. The pattern satisfies a psychological desire for clear antagonists and simple explanations.

## Human signal

The mind learns comfort in clarity: someone must be the monster so the story makes sense.

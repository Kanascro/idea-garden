## Core idea

The category of the “villain” is a storytelling device that culture mistakes for a real type of human being.

## Structure

Stories train us to sort the world into heroes and villains, but this binary is a narrative convenience rather than a description of human psychology. Harm emerges from pain, fear, scarcity, disconnection, and unprocessed trauma, not from a class of inherently evil people. Natural disasters show the same principle: destructive events occur without moral intention. Harm can be real without requiring mythologized antagonists.

## Human signal

Dissolving the villain does not excuse harm. It removes the costume that makes human behavior simpler than it is.

## Core idea

Modern society has progressively shifted from embedded belonging toward increasing individual separation and autonomy.

## Structure

Earlier social systems emphasized tight-knit identity—family, religion, community—where roles and meaning were predefined. Over time, individualism expanded, prioritizing personal freedom, self-definition, and mobility. This evolution increases optionality but also reduces default support structures, pushing individuals to construct meaning and connection themselves.

## Human signal

Freedom grows, but so does the weight of holding oneself alone.

## Core idea

Pushed to its extreme, individualism trends toward isolation where all connections become optional and reversible.

## Structure

If every bond is voluntary and contingent, long-term stability weakens. Individuals may optimize for autonomy at the cost of interdependence, leading to highly self-contained lives. At this limit, society fragments into loosely connected agents navigating through temporary alignments.

## Human signal

Independence peaks, but risks becoming loneliness.

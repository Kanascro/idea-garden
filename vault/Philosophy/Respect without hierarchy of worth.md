## Core idea

Removing rankings of importance challenges deeply embedded assumptions about value and priority.

## Structure

Most systems rely on implicit hierarchies to make decisions—what matters more, who comes first. Radical respect destabilizes these shortcuts, requiring more nuanced, situational responses. It does not eliminate decision-making but changes its basis from predefined ranking to contextual sensitivity.

## Human signal

There is both freedom and disorientation in letting go of fixed importance.

## Core idea

The villain myth does not only shape how we judge others; it also shapes how we judge ourselves.

## Structure

If humanity is divided into good and evil people, individuals begin policing their own inner life. Parts of the self that feel angry, fearful, or selfish are treated as evidence of hidden wickedness. Shame replaces curiosity, and people fear becoming the villain of their own story.

## Human signal

The same myth that condemns others quietly turns inward.

## Core idea

Moral categories exist as shared constructions but are often misperceived as inherent features of reality.

## Structure

Concepts like right and wrong function as coordination tools, similar to money or institutions. They emerge from collective agreement and context, not from fixed properties of the world. The issue is not their existence, but the confusion between their usefulness and their ontological status, leading them to be treated as truths rather than inventions.

## Human signal

There is a desire to loosen the grip of inherited certainty without denying its past function.

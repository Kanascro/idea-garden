## Core idea

Human harm can be understood without invoking mythical categories of evil.

## Structure

Research and lived experience show that destructive behavior often emerges from trauma, fear, deprivation, and learned patterns of response. Social systems can create conditions that amplify violence or exploitation. Recognizing these dynamics does not remove accountability; it situates harm within causal contexts rather than moral mythology.

## Human signal

People can cause immense damage without becoming creatures from a dark fairy tale.

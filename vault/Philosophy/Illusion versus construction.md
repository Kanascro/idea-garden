## Core idea

Calling something an illusion emphasizes its lack of inherent reality, while calling it a construction emphasizes its contingent usefulness.

## Structure

Both terms point to non-objectivity, but differ in tone and implication. A construction can still serve adaptive or coordinating purposes, while an illusion suggests something outdated or misleading. The shift in language reflects a judgment: that certain constructs may no longer serve their environment and can be outgrown.

## Human signal

The stance is not neutral—it carries a quiet push toward abandoning what no longer feels alive.

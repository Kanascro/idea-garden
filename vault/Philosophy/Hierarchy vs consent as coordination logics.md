## Core idea

People may prefer different mechanisms for organizing collective action: some rely on hierarchical authority, while others rely on consent-based coordination.

## Structure

Hierarchical coordination concentrates decision power within designated roles or ranks, allowing directives to flow downward through authority. Consent-based coordination distributes decision-making among participants, where actions proceed through agreement rather than command. Both systems solve the problem of coordination, but they operate through different assumptions about how legitimacy and power should circulate.

## Human signal

The tension is not about right or wrong. It is about which form of coordination feels trustworthy, natural, and livable to each person.

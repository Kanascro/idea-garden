## Core idea

Consent includes the freedom not to respond, explain, or justify oneself.

## Structure

Questions often carry implicit expectations of answers. However, genuine consent extends to conversational space as well. Individuals may choose not to answer questions about personal matters, beliefs, or decisions. Accepting silence or refusal without pressure respects psychological boundaries and protects the individual’s right to privacy and emotional pacing. In this framework, dialogue becomes voluntary participation rather than obligation.

## Human signal

Respect is demonstrated when curiosity does not override another person’s right to remain private.
